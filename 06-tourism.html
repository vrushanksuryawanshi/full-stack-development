Formerly known as Aurangabad, Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar is a city where ancient rock-cut wonders meet Mughal grandeur and Maratha legacy. Home to the UNESCO World Heritage Ajanta and Ellora Caves, this city has drawn travelers for centuries. The city is also a gateway to the Marathwada region, known for its rich culture, vibrant silk weaving tradition, and warm, spicy cuisine that tells the story of its varied rulers and peoples.

Wander through the 52 gates (Baaraas Darwazas) that once guarded the old city, taste the famous Naukhanda Palace, and lose yourself in the intricate sculptures of Daulatabad Fort. Whether you are a history enthusiast, an art lover, or a curious traveler, this city rewards every type of explorer with layers of discovery.

Top Attractions

🏛️
Ajanta Caves
UNESCO World Heritage
30 rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments dating from 2nd century BCE, featuring breathtaking paintings and sculptures.
🗿
Ellora Caves
UNESCO World Heritage
100 caves spanning Hindu, Buddhist and Jain art, including the magnificent Kailasa Temple carved from a single rock.
🏰
Daulatabad Fort
Historic Fortress
An impenetrable 14th century fort atop a 200m conical hill, with a labyrinthine interior designed to confuse invaders.
🕌
Bibi Ka Maqbara
Mughal Monument
The "Taj of the Deccan," built by Aurangzeb for his queen, mirroring the design of the Taj Mahal in Agra.
🌊
Panchakki
Water Mill Complex
A 17th century hydraulic water mill complex with a stunning garden, large tank, and the dargah of a Sufi saint.
🦋
Salim Ali Lake
Nature Reserve
Named after India's beloved ornithologist, this lake and bird sanctuary is a haven for migratory birds.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit: October to March is ideal, when the weather is pleasant and cool. Monsoon (July–September) turns the surrounding landscape lush green, though some cave sites may have restricted access.

Getting There: Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar Airport connects to major Indian cities. The railway station has good connectivity to Pune, Mumbai, Hyderabad, and Delhi. The city is also accessible by national highways.

Local Cuisine: Don't miss Naan Khaliya, a slow-cooked mutton dish, along with Sheer Khorma, Tahri, and the famous Sambhajinagar biryani. The city's Mughlai influences are evident in its rich, aromatic food culture.

Shopping: The city is famous for Himroo and Paithani silk weaving. Visit the Aurangabad Silk Cluster or the main bazaar near the old city gates for authentic handloom textiles that make for exceptional souvenirs.

Accommodation: The city has a wide range from heritage havelis to international hotels. Staying near the old city gives you walking access to the gates and bazaars, while hotels near the highway are convenient for fort day trips.

Day Trips: Ajanta Caves are 100km away, while Ellora is just 30km. Lonar Crater Lake, one of the world's few saline meteorite craters, is 3 hours away and worth a detour for science enthusiasts.
