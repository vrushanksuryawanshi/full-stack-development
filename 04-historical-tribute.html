The Early Years

Born on February 19, 1630, at Shivneri Fort near Pune, Shivaji Bhosale was the son of Shahaji Bhosale and the devoted Jijabai. From his earliest years, Jijabai filled his heart with stories of Ramayana and Mahabharata, shaping his vision of a sovereign Hindu kingdom.

He captured his first fort, Torna, at the age of just 16, announcing to the world that a new power was rising in the Deccan. Under his mother's blessing and his own indomitable will, he began the Hindavi Swaraj — the self-rule of his people.

Master of Guerrilla Warfare

Shivaji transformed the rocky terrain of the Sahyadri mountains into his greatest weapon. His Ganimi Kava — guerrilla tactics — confounded the vast armies of the Mughal Empire and Adil Shah, winning battles against overwhelming odds.

Key Events in a Legendary Life

1646Captured Torna Fort — first conquest at age 16, marking the beginning of the Maratha empire's foundation.
1659Slew Afzal Khan in a daring encounter at Pratapgad, shattering the Bijapur Sultanate's confidence forever.
1666Made a daring escape from Aurangzeb's captivity in Agra, hidden in fruit baskets — one of history's most celebrated escapes.
1674Coronated as Chhatrapati at Raigad Fort, formally establishing the sovereign Maratha kingdom.
1680Passed into eternity on April 3rd, leaving behind 300+ forts, a powerful navy, and an empire that would outlast the Mughals.

The Eternal Legacy

More than three centuries later, Shivaji Maharaj remains the symbol of courage, justice, and the spirit of Maharashtrian identity. His forts still stand as silent sentinels across the Sahyadri range, whispering his stories to the wind.
